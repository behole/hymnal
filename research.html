Welcome to the Hymnal Studio Research Database

Mission Statement

WE BUILD AN ARK. Hymnal Studio is dedicated to the research and development of Emergent Experimental Design principles. Our work challenges the dominant Singularity Design paradigm and proposes alternative frameworks for object creation.

This database contains documentation of ongoing research projects, theoretical frameworks, and empirical findings in the field of Emergent Design. All work is conducted under the supervision of Jon Pastir (Explorer 1), Principal Investigator.

Key Research Areas

  • Cubic Growth Principles - Multi-directional form development
  • Nature-Machine Unity Theory - Biological/mechanical system equivalence
  • 4-Corner Light Dynamics - Emission, transmission, reflection, absorption states
  • Oppositional Pairing - Design objects as cubic complements

Theoretical Framework

Emergent Experimental Design (EED)

Emergent Experimental Design represents a fundamental departure from conventional design methodology. Where traditional approaches begin with a singular concept and develop linearly (Singularity Design), EED allows form to arise from multiple simultaneous inputs.

"The Ark has four corners. The Ark contains all opposites. The Ark is emergent. The Ark is experimental. We build an Ark."
— J. Pastir, Foundations of Emergent Design, 2024

Critique of Singularity Design

Singularity Design (SD) dominates contemporary design education and practice. SD is characterized by:

  • Single-perspective development
  • Linear iteration processes
  • Reductive form simplification
  • Planned obsolescence accommodation

Research conducted at Hymnal Studio suggests that SD produces objects with reduced longevity, limited user resonance, and diminished aesthetic complexity compared to EED-produced objects.

Methodology

4-Corner Meditation Protocol

Primary research method involves 4-day meditation sessions with 4 materials in 4 positions under 4 light sources. Protocol developed by J. Pastir following initial revelation experience. Results in emergent form concepts arising from multi-input synthesis rather than singular ideation.

Experimental Controls

All Hymnal research maintains strict separation from Singularity Design influences:

  • No design school trained assistants
  • No industry-standard software
  • No trend-based material selection
  • No market-driven form decisions
